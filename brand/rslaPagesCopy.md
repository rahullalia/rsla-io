## 5. Case Study Writing Template (for Sanity CMS)

*(Use this template when creating new case studies in Sanity. Every case study should be reframed through the AI lens: what AI system was built, what manual work it replaced, what the measurable outcome was.)*

### Required Fields

**Title:** Short, results-focused. Lead with the outcome, not the client name.
- Good: "AI-Optimized Ads Turned $600 Into $36,000 in 45 Days"
- Bad: "Case Study: Local Service Company Marketing Campaign"

**Tag:** The primary AI service used. Use the new labels:
- `AI Lead Gen`
- `AI Automation`
- `AI Operations`
- `AI Digital Presence`

**Description:** 1 to 2 sentences. What was the business, what was the problem, what did AI do.

**Industry:** Select from the Sanity industry dropdown.

**Timeframe:** Number of days the engagement ran.

**Services Used:** Select from Sanity (update labels to match new names, see code updates section below).

**TL;DR (Executive Summary):** One paragraph. The entire story in 3 to 4 sentences. A busy founder should be able to read this and decide if they want the full story.

**Metrics:** 2 to 4 key numbers. Each with a value and label.
- Format values for impact: "$36,000" not "$36K", "80%" not "80 percent"
- Labels in uppercase mono: "CLOSED REVENUE", "REDUCTION IN CS HOURS"

**Key Takeaways:** 3 to 5 bullet points. Strategic shifts or insights. Not just "we ran ads." What was the thinking behind the approach?

**Body Content:** Full story in Portable Text. Follow this structure:

1. **The Situation** (1 to 2 paragraphs) — Who is the client? What business? What were they doing before?
2. **The Problem** (1 paragraph) — What manual work was killing them? What wasn't working? Be specific.
3. **The AI System We Built** (2 to 3 paragraphs) — What exactly did we build? Name the tools, the workflows, the AI components. Use the Space Grotesk callout blocks for technical details.
4. **The Results** (1 to 2 paragraphs) — Numbers. Specific, dated, verifiable. Use the statsCard block type for visual impact.
5. **What This Means** (1 paragraph) — The broader lesson. What can other founders learn from this?

**Related Cases:** Link 1 to 2 related case studies (Sanity handles this automatically if category matches).

### Voice in Case Studies

- Black Box tone on the website (confident, results-focused)
- But NOT corporate. Still sounds like Rahul wrote it.
- Specific numbers always. "$600 in spend" not "a small budget."
- Name the tools: "GoHighLevel", "Claude", "Meta Ads Manager"
- Show the dashboard. Include screenshots of actual results (stats cards, PortableText image blocks).

## 6. Blog Writing Guidelines

*(For writing blog posts that match the Mentor positioning and content pillars. All blogs are managed in Sanity CMS.)*

### Content Pillars (every post should fit one)

1. **"AI that actually works"** — Demystifying AI for founders. No hype, real examples. Tutorials, walkthroughs, tool reviews.
2. **"Behind the build"** — Showing AI systems built for clients (with permission). Screen recordings, step-by-step breakdowns, before/after.
3. **"The founder tech stack"** — Opinionated takes on tools, workflows, and what's worth paying for. Comparison posts, honest reviews.
4. **"Founder diaries"** — The real story of building RSL/A. Failures, wins, honest numbers. Vulnerability mixed with expertise.

### What NOT to Write About

- Generic marketing tips ("5 ways to improve your SEO")
- Anything that makes Rahul sound like every other marketing blog
- Pure news/trend commentary without a personal angle
- Anything without specific numbers, tools, or outcomes

### Post Structure (matches voice_dna_profile.md)

1. **Hook** — Specific moment or surprising statement. Not a definition or industry overview.
2. **Context** — What led to this moment. Set the scene.
3. **Challenge** — What wasn't working. Agitate the problem.
4. **Action** — What Rahul did. Be specific (tools, steps, decisions).
5. **Result** — Specific, quantified. "$600 into $36,000" not "great results."
6. **Insight** — What this means for the reader. The broader lesson.
7. **Engagement** — Question or call to action.

### Formatting Rules

- Subheadings as questions the reader might ask
- Short paragraphs (1 to 4 sentences max)
- Use the `callout` block for key insights
- Use the `code` block for anything technical (API calls, workflow configs)
- Use the `statsCard` block for numbers
- Use the `videoEmbed` block for Loom walkthroughs
- First mention of a tool should link out (affiliate or official URL)
- No dashes. Use "to" for ranges, ellipses for trailing thoughts.

### Categories (Sanity)

Update Sanity categories to match the new positioning. Suggested:
- `ai-automation` (keep)
- `ai-lead-generation` (new, replaces `paid-acquisition`)
- `ai-operations` (new, replaces `crm`, `go-high-level`)
- `founder-diaries` (new)
- `tools-and-tech` (new, replaces `business-tools`)
- `small-business` (keep)
