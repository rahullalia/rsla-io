## How do you implement conversational AI without it feeling robotic?

This is the question everyone asks. And it's the right question, because a bad bot is worse than no bot.

### Write like a human, not a help desk

The biggest mistake we see: bots that sound like corporate policy documents. "Thank you for reaching out! Your inquiry is important to us. A representative will be with you shortly."

Nobody talks like that. Your bot shouldn't either.

Here's what works:
- Use contractions ("we're", "you'll", "it's")
- Keep messages under 3 sentences
- Ask one question at a time
- Use the customer's name after they give it
- Match your brand voice (if you're a laid-back salon, your bot should sound laid-back)

### Set expectations immediately

Your first message should make clear that this is an AI assistant. Not to scare people away, but because transparency builds trust. Something like: "Hey! I'm the booking assistant for [Business Name]. I can help you find the right service and get you scheduled. What are you looking for?"

Most customers don't care that they're talking to AI. They care about getting their question answered quickly.

### Always have a human escape hatch

No matter how good your AI is, some conversations need a human. Build in clear escalation triggers:

- Customer says "talk to a person" or "real person"
- Sentiment turns negative (AI detects frustration)
- Question falls outside the bot's knowledge base
- Customer has been going back and forth for more than 5 exchanges without resolution

The handoff should be seamless. The human should see the full conversation, not ask the customer to repeat everything.

## What should you avoid?

- **Don't launch without testing.** Send 20 to 30 test conversations through the bot before going live. Try edge cases, weird phrasing, misspellings. Fix the gaps before real customers hit them.
- **Don't over-automate.** The bot should handle the routine stuff. The moment it tries to close a $10,000 deal over text, you've gone too far. Know where the human handoff belongs.
- **Don't forget to update the knowledge base.** Your menu changes, your pricing changes, your hours change. If the bot gives outdated information, it's worse than not having a bot.
- **Don't ignore the analytics.** GoHighLevel shows you every conversation, every drop-off point, every escalation. Review these weekly for the first month. Monthly after that. The bot improves when you feed it better data.
